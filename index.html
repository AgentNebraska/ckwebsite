Fighting waste, feeding people.

THE CAMPUS KITCHEN X FOOD RECOVERY NETWORK AT WASHINGTON UNIVERSITY IN ST. LOUIS FIGHTS TO SIMULTANEOUSLY ELIMINATE FOOD INSECURITY AND FOOD WASTE IN OUR COMMUNITY.

St. Louis is one of the most food insecure cities in the nation, with a food insecurity rate of 23.3%. Nationwide, 40% of food is wasted, feeding only the landfills. At CKxFRN WashU, we safely recover foods and ingredients, use them to prepare meals, and deliver them to our clients, who serve the most food insecure groups in St. Louis. Together, we can divert food waste and fight hunger in our own community.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

Our operations are split into three main categories: (1) Recovery, (2) Cooking, and (3) Delivery. All three are organized by our student organization's Leadership Team and carried out by our Wash U Shift Captains, who are experts in every process and lead each individual shift to ensure its success. On Cooking shifts, we rely also on volunteers, who can sign up online to lend a helping hand on a day that works for them. While our LT and Shift Captains are composed entirely of Wash U students, other members of the community are welcome to volunteer for a cooking shift, with no prior experience required!

1. RECOVERY
Recovery is the most immediate way that we can fight against food waste on a large scale. At CKxFRN WashU, we partner with Operation Food Search, a St. Louis non-profit and the largest distributor of free food in the bi-state region. They partner with enormous grocery retailers like Schnucks and Dierbergs to redistribute food that are still edible and nourishing but can no longer be sold.
